# Venezuelan Hydrocarbons Law Framework

## Quick Reference for Oil & Gas Legal Practice

---

## Constitutional Foundation

### Article 12 CRBV - Subsoil Resources
> Los yacimientos mineros y de hidrocarburos, cualquiera que sea su naturaleza, existentes en el territorio nacional, bajo el lecho del mar territorial, en la zona económica exclusiva y en la plataforma continental, pertenecen a la República, son bienes del dominio público y, por tanto, inalienables e imprescriptibles.

**Key Principles:**
- State ownership of all hydrocarbons (dominio público)
- Inalienable and imprescriptible
- Includes territorial sea, EEZ, and continental shelf

### Article 302 CRBV - State Reservation
> El Estado se reserva, mediante la ley orgánica respectiva, y por razones de conveniencia nacional, la actividad petrolera y otras industrias, explotaciones, servicios y bienes de interés público y de carácter estratégico.

**Effect:** Oil industry reserved to the State by organic law

### Article 303 CRBV - PDVSA Ownership
> Por razones de soberanía económica, política y de estrategia nacional, el Estado conservará la totalidad de las acciones de Petróleos de Venezuela, S.A., o del ente creado para el manejo de la industria petrolera, exceptuando las de las filiales, asociaciones estratégicas, empresas y cualquier otra que se haya constituido o se constituya como consecuencia del desarrollo de negocios de Petróleos de Venezuela, S.A.

**Key Points:**
- 100% state ownership of PDVSA required
- Exception for subsidiaries and strategic associations
- Mixed companies (empresas mixtas) permitted

## Ley Orgánica de Hidrocarburos (LOH)

### Publication
Gaceta Oficial No. 38.493, 04-08-2006

### Scope (Article 1)
Regulates all activities related to:
- Exploration
- Exploitation
- Collection
- Transportation
- Initial storage
- Domestic and international commercialization

Of liquid hydrocarbons (petroleum and derivatives).

### State Reservation (Article 9)
Primary activities are reserved to the State:
- Exploration
- Extraction
- Collection
- Initial transportation
- Initial storage

### Empresas Mixtas (Article 22)

The State may create mixed companies for primary activities with:
- **Minimum state participation: 50% + 1 share**
- Private sector participation permitted up to 49.99%

**Constitutional requirement:** State must maintain controlling interest

### Fiscal Regime

#### Royalty (Article 44)
- **30% minimum** on extracted hydrocarbons
- May be increased by Executive
- Calculated on wellhead value

#### Income Tax
- As per Ley de Impuesto Sobre la Renta
- Special provisions for oil sector

#### Surface Tax (Impuesto Superficial)
- Annual tax on exploration/exploitation areas
- Rates established by regulation

#### Extraction Tax
- Additional to royalty
- Linked to oil prices

#### Export Registration Tax
- 0.1% on export value

## Ley de Hidrocarburos Gaseosos

### Scope
Regulates non-associated natural gas (gas libre)

### Key Difference from LOH
- Less restrictive than oil regime
- Private participation more flexible
- Separate licensing regime

## Key Legal Issues in Hydrocarbons Practice

### 1. Empresas Mixtas Compliance
- Verify 50%+ state participation
- Board composition requirements
- Venezuelan law as governing law
- Caracas as dispute resolution forum

### 2. Fiscal Obligations
- Royalty calculations
- Tax optimization (within legal limits)
- Foreign exchange compliance
- ISLR obligations

### 3. Constitutional Challenges
- Any arrangement reducing state participation below 50%+1
- Privatization attempts
- Foreign arbitration clauses (limited)

### 4. Contract Requirements
- Gaceta Oficial publication
- Congressional approval (certain cases)
- Environmental permits
- Labor law compliance (LOTTT)

### 5. Nullity Risks
- Contracts violating Art. 302/303 CRBV
- Unauthorized state participation reduction
- Improper licensing
- Environmental violations

## Quick Reference: Prohibited Arrangements

1. **Private majority ownership** in primary activities
2. **Foreign law** as governing law for Estado activities
3. **International arbitration** for sovereignty matters
4. **Perpetual concessions**
5. **Transfer of reserved activities** to private parties
6. **Reduction of state participation** below 50%+1 in existing empresas mixtas
