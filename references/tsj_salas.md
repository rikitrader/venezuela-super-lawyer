# TSJ Chambers (Salas) Reference Guide

## Tribunal Supremo de Justicia - Structure and Jurisdiction

The Tribunal Supremo de Justicia (TSJ) is the highest court in Venezuela, established under the 1999 Constitution. It comprises seven specialized chambers (Salas).

---

## Sala Constitucional

### Jurisdiction
- Maximum interpreter of the Constitution (Art. 335 CRBV)
- Concentrated constitutional control
- Binding constitutional interpretations
- Review of amparo decisions
- Constitutional conflicts between state powers

### Key Competences (Art. 336 CRBV)
1. Nullity of national laws (total or partial)
2. Nullity of state constitutions and laws
3. Nullity of municipal ordinances
4. Nullity of executive acts with rank of law
5. Pre-ratification review of international treaties
6. Review of emergency decrees
7. Constitutional omissions
8. Constitutional interpretation conflicts
9. Controversies between constitutional bodies
10. Extraordinary review of amparo judgments

### Procedural Notes
- Writs: Acción de nulidad por inconstitucionalidad
- Amparo constitucional (original and appeal)
- Avocamiento (case assumption from lower courts)
- Revisión constitucional

### Binding Effect
**Interpretations by Sala Constitucional are binding on all other TSJ chambers and lower courts (Art. 335 CRBV).**

---

## Sala Político-Administrativa

### Jurisdiction
- Administrative law matters
- State contracts
- Tax disputes
- Regulatory matters
- Public service concessions

### Key Competences
1. Nullity of administrative acts of national scope
2. Disputes involving the Republic as party
3. Tax controversies (Código Orgánico Tributario)
4. Public contracts of national interest
5. Responsibility of high officials
6. Electoral administrative matters

### Typical Cases
- Nullity of ministerial resolutions
- Tax appeals from SENIAT
- Regulatory disputes with superintendencies
- Public procurement challenges
- Expropriation disputes

### Procedural Law
- Ley Orgánica de la Jurisdicción Contencioso Administrativa
- Código Orgánico Tributario (tax matters)

---

## Sala de Casación Civil

### Jurisdiction
- Civil cassation appeals
- Commercial/mercantile cassation
- Agrarian matters
- Family law (non-criminal)
- Succession and inheritance

### Key Competences
1. Cassation appeals in civil matters
2. Interpretation of civil/commercial law
3. Conflict resolution between civil courts
4. Regulation of competence

### Grounds for Cassation
- Violation of law (infracción de ley)
- Procedural defects (vicios de forma)
- Incongruity of judgment
- Immotivation
- Ultrapetita / citrapetita

### Procedural Law
- Código de Procedimiento Civil
- Código Civil
- Código de Comercio

---

## Sala de Casación Penal

### Jurisdiction
- Criminal cassation appeals
- Habeas corpus (certain cases)
- Criminal procedure interpretation
- Conflicts between criminal courts

### Key Competences
1. Cassation in criminal matters
2. Avocamiento in criminal cases
3. Regulation of criminal court competence
4. Interpretation of criminal procedure
5. Extradition matters

### Grounds for Cassation
- Violation of penal law
- Violation of procedural law
- Omission of essential formalities
- Contradiction in judgment

### Procedural Law
- Código Orgánico Procesal Penal (COPP)
- Código Penal

---

## Sala de Casación Social

### Jurisdiction
- Labor law matters
- Social security disputes
- Agrarian matters (labor aspects)
- Minor's protection (labor)

### Key Competences
1. Labor cassation appeals
2. Social security disputes
3. Labor law interpretation
4. Conflicts between labor courts
5. Collective bargaining disputes (certain)

### Special Features
- Pro-worker interpretation principle (in dubio pro operario)
- Abbreviated procedures
- Free access to justice in labor matters

### Procedural Law
- Ley Orgánica Procesal del Trabajo
- Ley Orgánica del Trabajo, los Trabajadores y las Trabajadoras (LOTTT)

---

## Sala Electoral

### Jurisdiction
- Electoral matters
- Political rights
- Electoral organization disputes
- Political party matters

### Key Competences
1. Electoral disputes (recursos electorales)
2. Nullity of electoral acts
3. Political party registration disputes
4. Electoral organization matters
5. Referendum disputes

### Procedural Notes
- Expedited procedures during electoral periods
- Precautionary measures in electoral matters
- Coordination with CNE (Consejo Nacional Electoral)

### Procedural Law
- Ley Orgánica del Poder Electoral
- Ley Orgánica de Procesos Electorales

---

## Sala Plena

### Jurisdiction
- Matters requiring full court participation
- High official trials
- Conflicts between TSJ chambers
- Special constitutional matters

### Key Competences
1. Trial of high officials (President, Vice-President, etc.)
2. Conflicts between TSJ Salas
3. Administrative matters of TSJ
4. Designation of lower court judges
5. Special matters assigned by law

### Composition
- All magistrates of TSJ sitting together
- Presided by TSJ President

## Case Assignment Flowchart

```
Legal Issue
    │
    ├─► Constitutional question? ─────────────► Sala Constitucional
    │
    ├─► Administrative/Tax matter? ───────────► Sala Político-Administrativa
    │
    ├─► Civil/Commercial cassation? ──────────► Sala de Casación Civil
    │
    ├─► Criminal cassation? ──────────────────► Sala de Casación Penal
    │
    ├─► Labor/Social Security? ───────────────► Sala de Casación Social
    │
    ├─► Electoral dispute? ───────────────────► Sala Electoral
    │
    └─► High official trial / Inter-Sala conflict? ─► Sala Plena
```

## Citation Format for TSJ Decisions

### Standard Format
```
TSJ/[Sala], Sentencia No. [número], Expediente [número], [fecha].
Ponente: [nombre del magistrado].
```

### Example
```
TSJ/SC, Sentencia No. 1, Expediente 00-1529, 20-01-2000.
Ponente: Jesús Eduardo Cabrera Romero.
```

### Short Citation
```
TSJ/SC, Sent. 1/2000
TSJ/SPA, Sent. 00647/2010
```
